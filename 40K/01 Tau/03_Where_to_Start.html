To answer the questions:

Get a riptide first. for $85 you get a 280 point model if optimized that's one of the best units in the game, and would be your big anti tank weapon. If you're fighting primaris the Heavy Burst Cannon with ATS is S6 -2AP with 2 Damage, so it'll mulch them fine. Use some extra drone bits from your Start Collecting to make the extra drones shield drones.

Crisis suits aren't good right now because they cost too much. They're still fun and can be good in the future. Magnetize those weapons for future changes.

I wouldn't get a second box. You have an Etheral and Suit team already, so a box of fire warriors is a good choice.

The main objective is to get a Battalion, and then get the Tau essentials, so the order of business is 1. Fire Warriors, 2. Cadre Fireblade or a Coldstar Commander 3. Pathfinders 4. Riptide. After that you can start specializing what units you really want to use, either Hammerheads, Broadsides, more riptides or lots of fire warriors.

Whole units to avoid: not a lot. Piranhas are kind of weak. Kroot Shapers and Krootox Riders suck. The Tidewall Shieldline and Tidewall Gunrig are pretty poor.

Crisis suits aren't bad per se, but they're pretty expensive, need support, and are a bit of a glass cannon unit. They also only really have three viable loadouts, flamers, cyclic ion, and fusion blasters (last one is insane overkill against most armies). Also get out competed by Commanders quite heavily.

Tau have tons of different guns, and not surprisingly there's a bunch of bad wargear options in there:

Pulse carbines on fire warriors are almost strictly worse than pulse rifles. Railguns on Hammerheads are strictly worse than ion cannons. Ion accelerator Riptides are not much better than heavy burst cannons, even against vehicles, but costs a lot more and isn't useful on smaller targets. There's virtually no reasons to take burst cannons (just take more fire warriors) or plasma rifles (just take ion blasters) on Crisis suits.
